About Steve Wozniak

Steve Wozniak was born on August 11, 1950 in Los Gatos, California and grew up in Sunnyvale, California. He was inspired to learn with a few science fair projects because his father was an engineer for Lockheed. Wozniak studied engineering at University of California at Berkeley where he first met Steve Jobs, who would become his best friend and future business partner.

After the creation of the Apple I and the Apple II, Steve Wozniak was awarded the National medal of Technology by the President of the United States in 1985, the highest honor bestowed on America's leading innovators. In 2002, he was induced into the Inventor's Hall of Fame and also awarded the prestigious Heinz Award for Technology, The Economy and Employment for the following " Single-Handedly designing the first computer and for then redirecting his lifelong passion for mathematics and electronics toward lighting the fires of excitement for education in grade school students and their teachers."
